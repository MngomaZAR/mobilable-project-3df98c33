# Papzi First 100 Users Operations

## Objective

Operate Papzi safely and manually for the first 100 users while learning which parts of the marketplace need automation next.

## Launch control model

Papzi should not behave like an open marketplace in week 1. It should behave like a curated concierge marketplace with software support behind it.

The operator goal is simple:

1. recruit a tight supply base
2. convert a small number of real client requests
3. manually keep response time, quality, and payouts reliable
4. only automate what becomes repetitive

## Who to recruit first

Recruit photographers before trying to scale broader creator categories. The first supply cohort should be easy to trust, easy to schedule, and able to fulfill common booking types.

### Priority supply mix

1. portrait and lifestyle photographers
2. family and couple photographers
3. event photographers for birthdays and small functions
4. one or two wedding-capable photographers for higher-value demand

### Ideal first cohort

- 10 approved photographers in one city first
- 6 highly responsive core photographers
- 2 premium photographers for higher-ticket jobs
- 2 backup photographers for overflow, reschedules, and no-shows

### Selection criteria

- replies within 15 minutes during test outreach
- has at least 8 strong portfolio images
- has WhatsApp and is comfortable with manual coordination
- can commit to 2 or more availability windows per week
- has clean KYC and payout details
- is willing to accept early platform-assisted jobs while the marketplace is still curated

### Avoid at the beginning

- providers who only reply late at night
- providers with inconsistent portfolio quality
- providers who negotiate every instruction manually
- providers who cannot commit to a service area
- providers who are not payout-ready

## 14-day launch control plan

### Days 1-3: seed supply

- recruit 10 photographers in Durban only
- complete KYC and payout setup for all of them
- manually verify location, style, and response time
- create an internal shortlist of the top 6 reliable providers

### Days 4-7: generate first bookings

- start direct outreach to likely clients
- use operator-assisted matching instead of open self-serve discovery
- keep every lead in a simple queue with owner, status, and next action
- personally confirm each booking before payment

### Days 8-10: tighten conversion

- identify where users drop: signup, booking form, payment, provider acceptance, or completion
- shorten time-to-first-response to under 5 minutes
- push one launch offer only if needed to close first transactions
- gather testimonial and referral consent from the first successful bookings

### Days 11-14: stabilize repeatability

- track which providers convert and complete best
- reduce poor-fit supply
- create repeat-booking follow-up for every successful client
- decide what belongs in the week-1 operator dashboard

## Daily operating cadence

### Morning

- review pending KYC
- review missing payout methods
- review failed or abandoned payments
- review same-day bookings and provider coverage
- follow up on overnight support issues

### Midday

- work the lead queue
- send provider availability check-ins
- confirm open jobs still have at least 2 backup providers
- resolve any booking at risk of delay

### Evening

- reconcile completed bookings vs payments and earnings
- confirm escrow releases needing action
- log manual interventions
- note product friction observed during the day

## Daily outreach script

### Photographer outreach DM or WhatsApp

Hi [Name], I’m building Papzi, a South Africa-focused platform for booking photographers quickly. We’re onboarding a small first group of trusted photographers in Durban and manually sending real client jobs to the most responsive creators first. If you’re open, I’d like to onboard you this week and send a test booking flow so you can see how it works.

### Follow-up if no reply after 24 hours

Hi [Name], following up in case you missed this. We’re keeping the first wave small on purpose, so I only need a few photographers who are reliable, payout-ready, and comfortable with WhatsApp coordination while we launch. Want me to send the onboarding steps?

### Client outreach

Hi [Name], we’re opening early access to Papzi for fast photographer booking in Durban. If you need a portrait, family, event, or couple shoot, I can personally match you with an available vetted photographer and confirm pricing before you pay. Want me to send 2 or 3 options?

### Client conversion follow-up

I’ve shortlisted photographers who fit your request. If you send your preferred date, area, and budget, I’ll confirm availability and lock the best option for you today.

## Photographer onboarding flow

### Step 1: qualify

- collect full name, city, WhatsApp number, service types, and portfolio link
- confirm service radius and typical pricing
- test response time

### Step 2: activate account

- create or verify Papzi account
- complete profile basics and portfolio
- complete KYC review
- add payout method

### Step 3: operator validation

- review portfolio manually
- verify role and profile row
- confirm availability windows
- tag photographer as `core`, `premium`, or `backup` in the operator sheet

### Step 4: dry run

- send a mock inquiry
- measure response time
- confirm they understand acceptance, arrival, completion, and payout flow

### Step 5: live ready

- mark as launch-ready
- add to active shortlist
- include in manual dispatch rotation

## Manual matching and fake-liquidity strategy

Do not fake supply with false availability. Instead, create the feeling of a responsive marketplace through operator-assisted matching.

### Allowed strategy

- show only vetted creators
- manually shortlist 2 to 3 photographers per lead
- confirm availability over WhatsApp before promising the job
- use operator language like "I’m confirming the best available option for you now"
- keep backup providers ready before sending the final quote

### Not allowed

- showing creators as available if they have not confirmed
- claiming instant matching when there is no operator coverage
- allowing users to pay before provider viability is checked for high-risk bookings

### Manual dispatch rule

- every booking should have 1 primary provider and 2 backups before payment is treated as safe
- if no provider accepts within 10 minutes, operator intervenes manually

## First-booking conversion playbook

### Goal

Turn each serious inquiry into a paid booking with minimal back-and-forth.

### Operator sequence

1. respond within 5 minutes
2. collect date, location, category, duration, and budget
3. shortlist 2 to 3 vetted photographers
4. confirm provider willingness manually
5. send one clear recommendation with price and next step
6. stay present through payment until booking is confirmed

### Recommended offer structure

- one recommended option
- one safer budget option
- one backup premium option only if the lead is high intent

### Conversion rule

Do not overwhelm the client with too many choices. Curated matching converts better than a long list in the first 100 users phase.

## Success metrics for the first 100 users

- lead response time
- signup to first booking conversion
- payment completion rate
- dispatch acceptance rate
- booking completion rate
- repeat booking rate
- support first-response time
- KYC review turnaround time
- payout turnaround time

## Exit criteria before scaling automation

- 20 or more completed bookings with no unresolved money incidents
- repeat bookings are starting to appear
- provider no-shows are rare and have a clean recovery path
- manual support load is understandable and documented
- the same manual checks are repeated daily and justify dashboard automation
